#3 Pork and Poblano Tacos

Ingedients:

  • Garlic
  • Roman Tomato
  • Yellow Onion
  • Poblano
  • Southwest Spice
  • Jalapeno
  • Lime
  • Ground Pork
  • Sour Cream
  • Flour Tortillas
  • Kosher Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Vegetable Oil

Cooking Instructions:

  1. step one was and dry all produce. Finely dice tomato. Peel and finely chop onion. finely chop jalapeno, removeing ribs and seeds if you prefer less heat. Quarter lime. Peel and finely chop garlic. Halve, core, and thinly slice poblano into strips
  2. In a small bowl, combine tomato, 2 TBSP onion, 2 tsp jalapeno, and a big squeeze of lime juice. Taste and season with salt and pepper
  3. in a second small bowl, combine sour cream a pinch of garlic, and squeeze of lime juice. Taste aned season with salt, pepper, and more lime juice if you like a tangy punch.
  4. Heat a large drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add poblano; cook, stirring, until softened and lightly charred, 5-6 minutes. Stir in remaining onion and garlic; cook, stirring, until softened, 3-4 minutes. Add pork and season with Southwest Spic, salt, and pepper. Cook, breaking up meat into pieces, until pork is browned and cooked through, 4-6 minutes. Turn off heat.
  5. While pork cooks, wrap tortillas in damp papper towels and microwave until warm and pliable, 30 seconds.
  6. Divide tortillas between plates and fill with pork mixture, pico de gallo, and garlicky sour cream. Serve with any remaining lime wedges on the side.

#2 Pork Sausage and Roasted Tomato Risotto

Ingedients:

  • Garlic
  • Roma Tomato
  • Yellow Onion
  • Chives
  • Pork Sausage
  • Arborio Rice
  • Shredded Parmesan
  • Chicken Stock Concentrate
  • Kosher Salt
  • Balck Pepper
  • Vegtable Oil
  • Butter

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees. Wash and dry all produce. cut tomatoes int half inch thick wedges, halve, peel, and dice onion. Peel and finely chop garlic. Finely chop chives. in a liquid measuring cup or large bowl, combine 4 cups hot water stock concentrate, and a big pinch of salt
  2. Toss tomatoes on one side of a baking sheet with a drizzle of oil, salt, and pepper. Place sausage on empty side. Rosat until tomatoes are browned and softed and sausage is browned and cooked thruogh, 20-25 minutes for sausage.
  3. Meanwhile, heat a drizzle of oil and 1 TBSP butter in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add onion and season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, until softeded, 4-5 minutes. Add garlic and rice; cook until garlic is fragrant and rice is translucent, 1 minute Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Add half cup stock to pan with rice mixture. Cook, stirring, until liquid has mostly absorbed. Repeat with remaining stock- adding half cup at a time and stirring until liquid has mostly absorbed- until rice is al dente and mixture is creammy, 25-30 minutes. Turn of heat
  5. Once tomatoes and sausage are done carfully transfer to cutting board. Once cool enough to handle, thinly slice sausage into rounds and roughly chop tomatoes.
  6. Once risotto is done, stir in chopped tomatoes, Parmesan, 1 TBSP butter and half the chives. Taste and season with salt and pepper. Divide between bowls and top with sliced sausage and remaining chives.

Mozz-Stuffed Caprese Burgers

Ingedients:

  • Yukon golden potatoes
  • Roma Tomato
  • Mayonnaise
  • Ground Beef
  • Potato Buns
  • italian Seasoning
  • Pesto
  • Sour Cream
  • Mozzarelle Chesse
  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper
  • Olive oil

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Adjust rack to top postition and preheat oven to 450 degrees. wash and dry all produce. cut potatoes into half inch thick wedges, toss on a baking sheet with 2 TBSP olive oil. half the Italian Seasoning and a few big pinches of salt and pepper. Roast on top rack until tender and crisped 20-25 minutes
  2. While potatoes roast. slice tomato into half inch think rounds
  3. In a small bowl. combine mayonnaise, sour cream, and half the pesto Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  4. In a medium bowl, combine beef Italian seasoning. and remaining pesto. Season generously with salt and pepper. Form mixture into two half inch think rounds. Divide mozzarella between the centers of each round. Gently fold meat around mozzarella, shaping and sealing to create cheese-stuffed patties, each slightly wider than a burger bun.
  5. Season tomato with salt and pepper. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large, perferably nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Add tomato and cook until lightly browned 1-2 minutes cook until lightly browned. 1-2 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate. Add another drizzle of olive oil to same pan; add patties and cook to desired doneness, 3-5 minutes per side.
  6. Meanwhile, toast buns. Spread top buns with creamy pesto; fill buns with patties and griddled tomato. Divide burgers between plates. Serve with potatoes and any remaining creamy pesto on the side.
